The Complete Poem

Orange Constellation:
The year my dad quit his job at the Christian school, biology and Bible, he packed boxes and buckets of fossils fished from Peace River into a storage unit.
White Constellation:
Whale bones and mammoth teeth and ammonites, a review of flesh turned to rock in a surreal miracle.
Yellow Constellation:
"Millions of fossils/ buried in rock layers, laid down by water/ all over the earth."
Blue Constellation:
Age 5, I composed a six-foot jumbo-size puzzle, sun's edge to the left, Pluto on the right.
Red Constellation:
He taught me it took God as long as me to assemble the solar system, the stars, that words take little time to make miracles, making and unmaking.
